Horse Buying Guide

Everything you should know before buying a horse. Make a smart and confident purchase.

🐎 Choose the Right Breed

Different horse breeds serve different purposes. Marwari horses are popular for ceremonies and endurance, Arabian horses are famous for stamina, Kathiawari horses adapt well to hot climates, and Thoroughbreds are ideal for racing and speed.

🩺 Check Horse Health

A healthy horse should have clear eyes, strong hooves, smooth skin, and good posture. Always inspect legs, joints, and breathing. A veterinary check is highly recommended.

📅 Verify Horse Age

The age of a horse can be estimated from its teeth. Younger horses are easier to train, while older horses may already have riding experience.

🧠 Temperament & Behavior

Observe how the horse behaves around people. A calm horse is ideal for beginners, while aggressive horses require experienced riders.

🏇 Test Ride

Always ride the horse before buying. A test ride helps evaluate comfort, speed, balance, and how the horse responds to commands.

📄 Verify Ownership Documents

Check registration papers, lineage certificates, and vaccination records. This ensures legal ownership and health history.

Things to Check Before Buying a Horse

  • Check hooves for cracks or infections
  • Observe the horse walking and trotting
  • Look for scars or swelling
  • Inspect teeth to estimate age
  • Ensure proper vaccination records
  • Verify lineage and breeder information
  • Check response to rider commands
  • Observe behavior around strangers

Factors Affecting Horse Price

Breed Rare breeds like Marwari and Arabian are more expensive
Training Professionally trained horses cost significantly more
Age Younger horses usually have higher value
Lineage Horses from champion bloodlines cost higher
Health Healthy horses with medical records are more valuable

Monthly Horse Maintenance

Owning a horse requires continuous care and financial commitment. Typical expenses include:

  • Quality feed and nutrition
  • Stable maintenance
  • Veterinary care
  • Training and exercise
  • Grooming equipment

Average monthly maintenance may range between ₹10,000 – ₹40,000 depending on breed and location.

Common Mistakes Buyers Make

  • Buying a horse without a health inspection
  • Ignoring the horse’s temperament
  • Not checking proper documents
  • Choosing a horse beyond riding experience
  • Underestimating maintenance costs
